# Smart-City Traffic Analysis

Data mining on 1.45M NYC taxi trips (JanтАУJun 2016) enriched with daily weather, covering
three tasks: congestion-level **classification**, traffic-regime **clustering**, and
**anomaly detection**.

## Preprocessing

- Weather CSV cleaned (mixed date formats, `T` trace values тЖТ 0.01) and merged onto trips by date.
- Haversine distance from pickup/dropoff coordinates; `speed = distance / duration` (km/h).
- Filtered: non-positive durations and passenger counts, coordinates outside NYC bounds
  (40.5тАУ41.0 N, тИТ74.5 to тИТ73.0 W), speeds outside 0тАУ120 km/h.
- Snowfall collapsed to a binary `is_snow` indicator (highly sparse).
- 1,458,644 тЖТ **1,452,287** rows retained.

## Results

**Classification** тАФ 3-class congestion label derived from speed terciles
(Q33 = 10.33 km/h, Q66 = 15.69 km/h). Speed excluded from the feature set to avoid leakage.
Trained on a stratified 100k sample.

| Model | Accuracy |
|---|---|
| Random Forest | **63.1%** |
| Logistic Regression | 44.4% |
| Random baseline | ~33% |

Per-class F1: High 0.68, Low 0.71, Medium 0.50. Medium is hardest тАФ it is the middle slice
of a continuous distribution, so boundary trips are not cleanly separable.
Feature importance: dropoff/pickup coordinates тЙИ 0.69 combined, `hour` 0.18.

**Clustering** тАФ K-Means, k=5, Calinski-Harabasz 189,400.

| Cluster | Mean speed | Mean hour | Reading |
|---|---|---|---|
| 4 | 11.1 km/h | 16.3 | Evening peak congestion |
| 0 | 14.6 km/h | 12.7 | Midday, elevated precipitation |
| 2 | 14.8 km/h | 14.8 | Afternoon, stable flow |
| 1 | 20.2 km/h | 4.8 | Early-morning free-flow |
| 3 | 27.9 km/h | 14.4 | Long/highway routes |

**Anomaly detection** тАФ Isolation Forest, `contamination=0.05`, 72,615 trips flagged.

The flagged trips are **long-haul airport transfers, not congestion events**:

- Median distance 15.3 km vs 2.0 km for normal trips
- 92% are *faster* than the normal median; only 4.5% are unusually slow
- 64.4% touch JFK/LGA/EWR vs a 6.8% base rate тАФ **9.5x over-representation**
  (JFK alone: 53.7% of anomalies vs 0.21% of normal trips)
- Anomaly rate peaks at 4тАУ5 AM (12тАУ15%) and bottoms out at 9 AM (2.9%) тАФ the inverse of
  a rush-hour-incident pattern

## Known limitations

- **Speed is a displacement rate, not a true speed.** Distance is straight-line haversine,
  so circuitous routes look slow even in free-flow traffic. Road-network distances
  (e.g. OSRM) would fix this. It affects every speed-derived result here, including the
  classification target.
- **Weather is daily, trips are hourly.** All trips on a given day share one temperature and
  precipitation value, so weather structurally cannot explain intra-day variation тАФ which is
  where congestion lives. Low weather feature importance is partly an artifact of this.
- `hour` and `day_of_week` are encoded as linear integers, so hour 23 and hour 0 sit far apart
  in the scaled clustering space. Cyclical (sin/cos) encoding is the standard fix.
- k=5 was chosen for interpretability, not selected via an elbow/silhouette sweep.
- K-Means vs MiniBatchKMeans is not a true algorithm comparison тАФ MiniBatch is a stochastic
  approximation of the same objective (and scored lower: CH 163,101 vs 189,400).
- Near-zero-displacement trips (round trips returning to the same point) survive the speed
  filter and land in the "High congestion" bucket, adding noise to the bottom tercile.
